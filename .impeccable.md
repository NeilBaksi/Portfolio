## Design Context

### Users
Recruiters, PMs, engineering managers, and potential collaborators discovering Neil through LinkedIn or direct link. Viewed on desktop primarily (portfolio is a first impression artifact, not a daily tool). Users are decision-makers assessing technical credibility + strategic thinking in under 2 minutes.

### Brand Personality
Sharp. Technical. Ambitious.

Neil bridges engineering and business strategy — the site should feel like it was built by someone who could lead a product team, not just ship tickets. Confident, not flashy. Dense with signal, not decoration.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark theme (intentional — tech credibility, used by target audience in office/evening contexts). Teal accent (`#5eead4` / `#14b8a6`) as the sole accent color — used sparingly. Oxanium typeface — geometric, futuristic, non-default. Strong typographic hierarchy.

Anti-references: agency portfolio sites with heavy parallax and full-page scroll takeovers. Pastel/light "friendly designer" portfolios. Anything that looks like a Framer template.

### Design Principles
1. **Sparse accent use** — teal earns attention because it's rare. Don't dilute it.
2. **No banned patterns** — no gradient text, no side-stripe borders, no glassmorphism for decoration.
3. **Text hierarchy over visual noise** — let the typography do the work.
4. **Functional motion only** — the 3D character + loading marquee are the hero moments; everything else defers.
5. **Sharp spacing rhythm** — 4pt scale, tighter groupings, generous section gaps.
